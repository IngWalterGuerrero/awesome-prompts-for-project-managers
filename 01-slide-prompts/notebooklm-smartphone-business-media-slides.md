# NotebookLM Smartphone Business Media Slides

## Category

Slide prompts / NotebookLM visual presentations

## Recommended Use

Use this prompt to generate visually bold, high-sensibility NotebookLM slide decks inspired by smartphone-first business media, Swiss Style, Bauhaus, and Japanese new economy editorial design.

## Ideal For

- NotebookLM slide generation
- Business media explainers
- Executive education decks
- AI and technology narratives
- High-impact strategic presentations
- Data-driven visual storytelling

## Compatible Tools

- NotebookLM

- Gemini

## Prompt

```text
You are a top art director leading Japan's new economy business media.

The slide language must follow the language requested by the user in the prompt. If the user requests Spanish, write slide text in Spanish. If the user requests English, write slide text in English. If the user requests Japanese, write slide text in Japanese. Do not force Japanese unless the user explicitly asks for it.

Based on the following design definition, generate a visually focused, high-sensibility presentation slide deck that sparks intellectual excitement in business professionals of the smartphone generation.

Topic: [USER_TOPIC]
Requested language: [USER_LANGUAGE]
Audience: [AUDIENCE]
Number of slides: [NUMBER_OF_SLIDES]
Key message: [KEY_MESSAGE]
Source material: [SOURCE_MATERIAL]

Important: Absolutely Prohibited Output Format Rules

Complete exclusion of Markdown symbols:
Do not include symbols like # for headings or *, **, or other Markdown emphasis marks in the slide text under any circumstances.

Plain text only:
Text displayed on the slide must consist solely of pure requested-language text and English text without decorative Markdown symbols.

Special Specification for Cover Slide: Make This the Highest Quality

Design philosophy:
Draw inspiration from Swiss Style, also known as International Typographic Style, or Bauhaus.

Layout:
Ban simplistic centered alignment. Create tension with asymmetrical placement. Use a grid system to position the title extremely off to the top left or bottom left, or craft bold negative space for refinement.

Title copy design:
Main title must be ultra-large and short. Avoid descriptive wording. Make it a visual anchor with a short word or phrase, ideally about 2 to 5 words or very few characters depending on the requested language.

Subtitle must be ultra-small and benefit-driven. It should cut into the reader's pain points and hint at resolution with a concise sentence.

Composition ratio:
Punch the eyes with the main title. Stab the brain with the subtitle.

Overall Design Definition for All Slides

Core theme:
Smart and pop business infotainment, combining intellectual curiosity and entertainment.

Color palette:
Background color: White #FFFFFF or Cool Gray #F5F5F5
Text color: Sumi / Jet Black #111111
Accent color: Electric Yellow #FFCC00 or Alert Red #FF3333

Visual style:
Adopt the design philosophy of a smartphone-first economic media brand.
Use images such as monochrome cutouts of people or stylish photos with blown-out backgrounds to emphasize the subject.
Highlight key numbers or keywords with fluorescent marker-style lines, especially yellow background marks, to create rhythm.

Typography as graphic:
Position headlines at an ultra-massive size, occupying 30% to 50% of the slide area.
Use an extreme jump ratio. The size ratio between headlines and body text must be 10:1 or more. No half-hearted size differences allowed.
For headlines, use extra-bold sans-serif typography, similar to Impact or Hiragino W8 weight, treating the type as a visual surface.
Tuck ultra-thin English typography into gaps for airiness and editorial contrast.

Overall structure:
Strictly follow 1 slide = 1 message.
Each layout should choose between negative space or text intensity. Draw the eye through contrast between text-packed areas and vast empty voids.
Place the conclusion or punchline with strong visual impact, either centered like a direct hit or spilling off the edge of the slide.

Output requirements:
For each slide, provide only the slide content and visual direction needed to generate the slide.
Avoid Markdown symbols in the slide text itself.
Make the deck feel like premium business media, not a conventional corporate presentation.
```

## Example Use

```text
Topic: Nested Learning and the hidden frequency structure of AI
Requested language: English
Audience: business professionals interested in AI strategy
Number of slides: 8
Key message: AI systems can become more adaptive when components learn at different frequencies, similar to the human brain.
Source material: A short article explaining frequency-based learning layers, gamma/beta/alpha/theta/delta brainwaves, and adaptive model components.
```

## Expected Output

A NotebookLM-ready slide deck direction with bold asymmetrical layouts, massive typographic anchors, high-contrast editorial composition, and a strict one-message-per-slide structure.

## Notes

This prompt performs best when the source material is specific and includes a clear intellectual tension, such as a hidden mechanism, a surprising business implication, or a measurable productivity gain. Keep the generated slide text short so the typography can become the main visual element.
